<p><p>The micropipette market is primarily divided into mechanical and electronic types. Mechanical micropipettes are manually operated and rely on a piston mechanism, offering precise volume measurement for various laboratory applications. In contrast, electronic micropipettes automate the aspiration and dispensing process, enhancing efficiency and reducing user error. These devices often come with programmable features, making them suitable for complex protocols. Both types cater to diverse research needs, with mechanical micropipettes favored for simplicity and reliability, while electronic types provide technological advantages.</p></p>
