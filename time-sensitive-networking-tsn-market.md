<p><p>Time-Sensitive Networking (TSN) enhances Ethernet by enabling deterministic and low-latency communication, crucial for applications like industrial automation, automotive, and audio/video streaming. Key standards include IEEE 802.1AS (time synchronization), IEEE 802.1Qca (path control), IEEE 802.1AS-Rev (enhancements to synchronization), IEEE 802.1Qbv (time-aware queuing), IEEE 802.1Qci (ingress shaping), IEEE 802.1CB (frame replication), IEEE 802.1Qcc (enhanced resource reservation), IEEE 802.1Qch (frame preemption), and IEEE 802.1CM (networking for time-sensitive applications), each addressing specific needs for reliable, synchronized data transmission.</p></p>
