Woven 3D fabrics are created by interlacing yarns, providing strength and flexibility, ideal for applications in aerospace and automotive industries. Orthogonal nonwoven fabrics involve layering fibers in different directions, enhancing performance while minimizing weight; these are commonly used in medical and automotive sectors. Other advanced structures incorporate innovative designs and materials, catering to specialized applications such as sportswear and protective gear.</p></p>
