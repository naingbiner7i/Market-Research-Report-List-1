<p><p>The cephalosporin market is categorized into four generations, each representing advancements in antibiotic effectiveness and spectrum. First-generation cephalosporins primarily target gram-positive bacteria, while second-generation varieties have broader coverage, including certain gram-negative strains. Third-generation cephalosporins offer enhanced activity against gram-negative bacteria and are often used for severe infections. Fourth-generation cephalosporins further expand antimicrobial spectrum and resistance to beta-lactamases, making them suitable for complicated infections. This progression reflects ongoing improvements in fighting bacterial resistance and tailoring treatments.</p></p>
