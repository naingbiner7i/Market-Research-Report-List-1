<p><p>The hyaluronic acid market is segmented into two primary types: microbial fermentation and animal tissue sources. Microbial fermentation involves using bacteria or yeast to produce hyaluronic acid, offering a more sustainable and ethically preferred option. This method allows for large-scale production with fewer regulatory concerns. Conversely, animal tissue-derived hyaluronic acid is extracted from sources like rooster combs or other animal tissues, traditionally valued for its purity but raises ethical and supply chain issues. Both types cater to various applications, including cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and joint health products.</p></p>
