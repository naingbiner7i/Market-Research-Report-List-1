<p><p>Injectable nanomedicines encompass various types, including liposomes, which are lipid-based carriers that encapsulate drugs for enhanced delivery and reduced toxicity. Micelles are formed from surfactants and improve drug solubility and stability. Nanocrystals increase bioavailability of poorly soluble compounds. Polymeric nanoparticles offer controlled release and targeted delivery through biodegradable materials. Other market types may include dendrimers and inorganic nanoparticles, each providing unique properties and applications in drug delivery, imaging, and therapeutic interventions.</p></p>
