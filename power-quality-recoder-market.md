<p><p>Power quality recorders are categorized based on their data acquisition capabilities, denoted by terms like 1G, 2G, 4G, and 16G. These "G" levels indicate the generation of technology and the recorder's sampling rate, with higher numbers representing enhanced capabilities. A 1G recorder captures basic power quality data, while 2G offers improved sampling for transient analysis. 4G devices provide more detailed data for complex events, and 16G recorders support high-resolution, high-speed monitoring, ideal for critical power system applications.</p></p>
